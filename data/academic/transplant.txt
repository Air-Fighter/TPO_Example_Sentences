Spartina was transported to Washington State in packing materials for oysters transplanted from the east coast in 1894.
Spartina has been transplanted to England and to New Zealand for land reclamation and shoreline stabilization.
Although raccoons are native only to the Western Hemisphere, they have been successfully transplanted to other parts of the globe.
As an example of the ease with which transplanted individuals can succeed, raccoons from Indiana (midwestern United States) have reportedly been able to flourish on islands off the coast of Alaska.
